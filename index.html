Caring for Orchids While in Bloom

(This page explains caring for orchids while in bloom. If your orchid has finished flowering, or you require more in depth potting instructions, please click here.)

Quick tips:

  • After purchasing an orchid, do not repot until flowering has finished.
  • Orchids require bright, indirect light. Direct sunlight will burn them.
  • Water orchids after soil has completely dried.

Overview

Most species of orchid will bloom once a year. When you purchase an orchid plant from the store, chances are it is already flowering. Orchids are often sold in medium that is unsuitable for the plant and can cause the roots to rot.

You may be tempted to repot your new plant as soon as possible, but is very important not to repot an orchid while it is in bloom.

Keeping your orchid healthy during the first bloom is important if you want these lovely flowers to come back year after year.

Watering

Orchids need to be watered once the potting medium has dried completely. Once dry, pour in room tempurature water to soak the plant.

Orchids are very susceptible to root rot, so it is important not to over water them.

You don't need to use an ice cube. I would reccomend against it.

Lighting

Orchids require bright, indirect sunlight to grow healthily. Direct sunlight can burn the leaves, causing your orchid to shrivel and die.

Climate

Orchids are tropical plants by nature, meaning they do best in warm, humid environments. If you live in a dry area, you can simulate this by misting the orchid with room tempurature water, avoiding the delicate flowers.
